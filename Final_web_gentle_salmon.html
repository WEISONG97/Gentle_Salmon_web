Question 2:
"So… Why does it look like this?
Maybe because of the socio-economic factors!"


🚩 Tips: People would usually choose the bike station within the shortest distance without considering about the administrative boarder.
Therefore, based on similar studies, I decide to use buffer of 500m distance and recalculate socio-economic variables.




Observe the economic differences, hover over for details！



🙋 Findings: In addition to exploring the unbalanced distribution of socio-economic conditions, I also conduct the multiple linear regression to understand the relationship between social-economic factors and total flows.
Variation inflation factors (VIF) are detected to avoid multicollinearity and improve model accuracy.
Finally, my model could explain 57.2% variables!


🚩 Tips: Stations that not be consistent with model results possibly influenced by other reasons such as spatial autocorrelation.
So I decide to visualise them geographically to check the outliers.

Like I do, you might notice that limited results for these factors can be found in some areas... It's totally normal!

So...How did it happen?


🚩 Tips: So I decide to switch to K-means clustering to figure out the relationships further.
I examine the flows as departure and destination to understand productivity and attractiveness of stations as data from the perspectives of employees and residents.
To optimise clustering results, I standardise data and use principal component analysis and choose number of clusters by Elbow and Silhouette score.

Advice for...
😉Aha, do you remember that the goal of this curious Journey is to protect our cities from motor vehicle pollution and to encourage people actively participating in the green travel team.
Above all, we can see that the shared bicycle business is difficult to develop in urban fringe areas, and most users are wealthy.
Now I would like to give some advice.
🏛 Government
    To Government


  • Conduct initiations to encourage enterprise to put bicycles in the suburbs.


  • Improve cycle lanes and regulate traffic rules, especially on routes with high use of shared bicycles.


  • Control the price of sharing bikes.

🏬 Companies

    To Companies:


  • Put more bikes on the suburb area.


  • Put more bikes in area with high employment density, especially for service and office industry.


  • Set different price in different areas. For some areas with low income people gathering, the price could be lower with some conditions.

🏤 NGOs and Others

    To NGOs and Others:


  • Propoganda to med/low income people, cars owners, people who are in industrial, entertainment and retail industries.


  • Actively support to governments about policy making of bike sharing.


  • Researchers could pay more attention on research considering socio economic factors based on above results.
